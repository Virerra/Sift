Module 03

03 Digital citizenship & etiquette

Safety is about what happens to a kid online. This module is about the other direction — how they treat other people, and what that leaves behind.

Tone

Text strips out tone

A joke that would land fine in person, said with the right face and pause, can read as genuinely cruel typed out flat with no emoji or context. Kids underestimate this gap constantly — not out of malice, just because they haven't yet felt the receiving end of it enough times to build the instinct. Worth naming directly: "would this be funny if you couldn't see my face when I said it?" is a genuinely useful filter, for adults too.

Conflict

Cyberbullying — on both sides

Most guidance here focuses on kids as targets, which matters. But it's worth an equally direct conversation about the other direction: group chats and comment threads make it easy to pile on without any one person feeling like the instigator — "everyone was doing it" is how a lot of cruelty online actually happens, not through one clearly bad kid.

If your kid is the target

Save the evidence before blocking or reporting — a screenshot survives even if the other person deletes the message. Most platforms have a reporting flow for harassment specifically, separate from ad reports; Module 07 covers where those live.

Permanence

A digital footprint is a real thing

"Delete" often means "hidden from the original poster," not "gone" — screenshots exist, platforms retain data, and things shared to even a small group can resurface years later in a context the original poster never imagined (a college application, a first job). This isn't a reason for fear, but it's a real enough mechanism to be worth a kid actually understanding, not just being told "be careful" without the underlying why.

Other people

Other people's privacy is also privacy

Posting a photo of a friend, tagging their location, or sharing a screenshot of a private conversation are all decisions being made on someone else's behalf, without asking. A simple habit worth instilling: before posting something involving another person, a quick "is it okay if I post this?" costs nothing and heads off a lot of avoidable conflict and hurt feelings.

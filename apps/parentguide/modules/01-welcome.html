Three short things before the rest of the guide: what SIFT actually is, what the next six modules cover, and — since this page tracks your reading — exactly what that means for your privacy.

What SIFT is

SIFT is a small set of open-source tools built around one idea: you should be able to see what ads are actually being shown to kids online, not just trust that platforms are handling it. The first piece, AdSentinel, is a browser extension that scans the ads on a page and flags ones matching known manipulative or age-inappropriate patterns — with a plain-English reason for every flag, and nothing sent anywhere unless you choose to share it.

Worth knowing going in

AdSentinel reads text — an ad's copy, its labels, which network served it. It cannot look at an ad's actual image or video. A clean scan means nothing text-based tripped a rule; it's not a guarantee the ad is fine. Module 06 covers this in more depth.

How this page tracks your progress

Each module is broken into a few sections, like this one. As you read — genuinely read, not just scroll past — a small dot fills in next to the section, and the ring in the corner fills in to match.

All of that lives in your browser's local storage, under one key namespaced to this site. It never leaves your device: no account, no server, nothing sent to SIFT. Clearing your browser data clears this progress too — there's nothing to recover, by design.

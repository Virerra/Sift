07 Spot it, report it

The practical payoff of everything before this. What to actually watch for, and exactly where a report goes — both to the platform, and to SIFT.

The quick mental checklist

Fake urgency or fake prizes — "only 2 left," "you've been selected," language built to skip a considered decision.

Age-restricted products on kid content — gambling, dating apps, alcohol, vaping, "get rich quick" schemes.

Anything that just feels wrong — you don't need to name why before it's worth reporting. Trust it.

Report #1

Report it to the platform directly

This is the report that can actually get a specific ad taken down. Usually behind a small "···" menu on the ad itself.

  • YouTube: the "···" menu on the ad, or Google's ad complaint form
  • Facebook / Instagram: the "···" menu on the ad → Report ad
  • TikTok: tap and hold the ad, or use the flag icon on it

Report #2

Report it to SIFT

This doesn't take the ad down — it's what turns one "this seemed off" into a pattern other people can see. Use AdSentinel's Export button, then open an issue with the ad report template and attach the export. A GitHub login is the only account this needs, and only for this specific step.

What actually happens after you report

Worth setting expectations honestly: platforms review reports, but review doesn't guarantee removal — plenty of reported ads are judged compliant and stay up. SIFT's issue tracker isn't a takedown mechanism either; it's how the heuristics ruleset gets better over time, and how AdDashboard's aggregate picture gets more accurate. Neither replaces the other — a report worth making is usually worth making in both places.
